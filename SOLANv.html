About Solan

Solan, famously called ‘The mushroom city of India’ and ‘City of Red Gold’ due to its abundant mushroom and tomato production is a beautiful city located amidst scenic snow-clad mountains and forests. It is famous for many things — from its cuisines to its scenery, Solan is a must visit place in Himachal Pradesh. It is a great place for trekking and it is heaven for those who love to feel oneness with nature.

Famous for: Culture, Cuisine, Heritage. Major Attraction: Shoolini devi temple, Dolanji Bon Monastery. Nearby Places: Shimla, Kasauli, Barog.


How to Reach

AIR:
Chandigarh is the nearest airport with good connectivity. It takes about 2 hr 14 min (66.8 km) from here to Solan via road.

RAIL:
Solan Railway station (SOL) lies on the Kalka Shimla Railway line. It lies on a narrow broad gauge and therefore is not accessible from all the major cities in India. Shimla Railway Station (SML) is the nearest station and visitors can catch a toy train from here to Solan. They can also hail prepaid cabs or buses from Shimla to Solan, 1 hr 40 min (46.1 km) away.

ROAD:
Himachal Road Transport Corporation (HRTC) operates regular buses to and from Solan to other cities in HP. Buses are also available from Delhi, Pathankot, Chandigarh. NH5 and Jammu – Delhi Rd connects Solan.
